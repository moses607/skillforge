# Go-to-market playbook

## Blunt reality (read first)
The generic dev/prod/marketing skill market competes with a large and growing pile of **free** skills (anthropics/skills, sales-skills, many community marketplaces). GitHub has **no payment layer** — you sell elsewhere and use the repo as a shop window. Winning here is a **distribution + trust** game, not a "make more skills" game. Quality and a channel beat quantity.

## The funnel
1. **Free pack on GitHub** = the storefront. Make it genuinely excellent so it gets starred/shared. Stars are your distribution.
2. **README sells the Pro pack** with a single clear CTA (Gumroad/Stripe link).
3. **Pro pack** delivered as a licensed download.
4. **Bespoke skills** = the real revenue, pitched to anyone who buys Pro.

## Getting the first 100 installs
- Publish the free pack; write a short "how I built 9 Claude skills" post (dev.to / your blog / LinkedIn).
- Post to: r/ClaudeAI, r/ClaudeCode, Hacker News (Show HN), the Claude Developers Discord, X.
- Add `claude-skills`, `claude-code`, `agent-skills` GitHub topics so the trending/search surfaces you.
- Ask 5 people who'd actually use one skill to try it and star the repo.

## Converting free → paid
- Every free skill's README footer links to the matching Pro skill ("liked `commit-craft`? `refactor-surgeon` plans the whole cleanup").
- Offer the Pro pack at a launch discount for the first N buyers.
- Collect emails (a simple "get notified of new skills" link) — your list is worth more than the repo.

## What NOT to do
- Don't ship 30 shallow skills to look big. 9 excellent + 9 premium that clearly outperform an improvised prompt is the whole game.
- Don't put Pro skills in the public repo "just to show them" — screenshots/descriptions sell; the files don't.
- Don't compete on price with free by being cheaper-and-worse. Compete on being visibly better + supported.

## The 90-day plan
- Weeks 1–2: polish free pack, publish, seed distribution.
- Weeks 3–6: launch Pro (Gumroad), gather feedback, fix triggering/descriptions on the skills that under-trigger.
- Weeks 7–12: land 1–3 bespoke-skill clients from Pro buyers. That's where a real business is, if there is one.
