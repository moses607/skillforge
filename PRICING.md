# Pricing strategy

## The honest constraint
A raw `SKILL.md` is a text file. Once someone downloads it, the IP is theirs to copy. So:
- **Never** put the Pro skills in a public repo.
- Sell them as a downloadable pack (Gumroad/Lemon Squeezy/Stripe) with a commercial license.
- Enforcement is legal + friction, not DRM. Price low enough that copying isn't worth the hassle; keep value in **updates + support**, which pirates don't get.

## Suggested tiers
| Tier | Price (indicatif) | Contents |
|------|-------------------|----------|
| Free | 0 | 9 free skills (this repo) — the funnel |
| Pro | $29–49 one-time | 9 premium skills + 12 months of updates |
| Team | $99–199 | Pro + org-wide license + priority requests |
| Custom | quote | A bespoke skill built for the buyer's workflow |

One-time beats subscription for a file-based product unless you host execution. Updates justify the price without a subscription treadmill.

## Where the money actually is (ranked)
1. **Bespoke skills** for a company's specific workflow (highest margin, low competition).
2. **Team licenses** to orgs already using Claude Code.
3. **The Pro pack** as volume/funnel.
4. Free pack = pure lead-gen. It is not revenue; it's the top of the funnel.

## Pricing rule
People don't pay for "how cool it is" — they pay for time saved or a mistake avoided. Anchor the price to the outcome (e.g. "one avoided bad architectural decision > $49"), not to line count.
